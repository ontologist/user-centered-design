Persona Development

Why Personas Are Critical for Success

üéØ Focus Development Efforts

Personas prevent feature creep by providing a clear answer to "Would [Persona Name] use this?" If the answer is no, don't build it.

ü§ù Build Stakeholder Empathy

When stakeholders disagree, referring to persona needs (backed by research) provides objective decision criteria instead of opinions.

üí° Drive User-Centered Decisions

Every design choice, feature prioritization, and trade-off discussion starts with: "What does our persona need and why?"

üìä Align Cross-Functional Teams

When IT, HR, and executives share the same understanding of who they're building for, collaboration improves dramatically.

‚úÖ Validate Requirements

Personas help test requirements: "Would this solve [Persona Name]'s problem? Is this how they would actually use it?"

üìà Measure Success

Success metrics tied to persona goals are more meaningful than generic KPIs. "Did we reduce Sarah's time to find HR info?"

Your current challenge: Eduardo is pushing for broad functionality while the team faces capacity constraints and unclear requirements.

How personas help: When Eduardo requests new features, you can respond with data: "Our research shows Employee Persona Sarah's top 3 needs are X, Y, Z. Feature A addresses need X (high priority). Feature B doesn't align with any persona need (deprioritize)."

This transforms subjective debates into objective, research-backed decisions.

Persona Development Best Practices

What Makes a Good Persona?

  • Research-Based: Derived from real user interviews, observations, and data - not assumptions
  • Specific: Detailed enough to feel like a real person, not a generic "user"
  • Goal-Oriented: Focused on what users want to accomplish, not just demographics
  • Limited in Number: 3-5 primary personas maximum (too many dilutes focus)
  • Actionable: Provides clear guidance for design and development decisions
  • Shared Understanding: Everyone on the team knows and references them

Common Persona Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Made-Up Personas

Creating personas from assumptions without user research leads to building for imaginary users.

Instead: Base every persona on at least 5-8 real user interviews

‚ùå Too Many Personas

Creating a persona for every possible user type dilutes focus and makes decision-making harder.

Instead: Identify 3-5 primary personas that represent the majority of use cases

‚ùå Demographics Over Goals

Focusing on age, location, job title without understanding motivations and needs.

Instead: Lead with goals, pain points, and behaviors. Demographics are supporting context.

‚ùå One-Time Creation

Creating personas once and never updating them as you learn more.

Instead: Treat personas as living documents that evolve with ongoing research
